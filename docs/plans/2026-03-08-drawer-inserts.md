# Drawer Insert Design — 75 × 32 × 12 cm

Cardboard inserts for a 75 × 32 × 12 cm (W × D × H) drawer.
Two-layer system: a fixed **bottom insert** and a **sliding top insert** that moves left-to-right.

## Design Concept

The top insert is **full-depth** (spans the entire 31 cm depth of the bottom insert) but **narrower** (58 cm wide), leaving ~16 cm of horizontal travel inside the bottom insert. This lets you slide the top insert left or right to access the bottom layer beneath. The exposed column of the bottom insert is always within arm's reach, and the insert never overhangs the drawer.

## Dimensions & Measurements

### Drawer Interior

| Dimension | Value |
|:----------|:------|
| Width     | 75 cm |
| Depth     | 32 cm |
| Height    | 12 cm |

### Bottom Insert (Base Tray)

Sits flat on the drawer bottom. The top insert slides on top of it left-to-right.

| Dimension | Value | Notes |
|:----------|:------|:------|
| Width     | 74 cm | 0.5 cm clearance on each side for easy drawer fit |
| Depth     | 31 cm | 0.5 cm clearance front/back |
| Wall height | 5 cm | Half the drawer height — top insert sits on top |

The bottom insert is a simple open box — no internal dividers. Use it for flat items, spare notebooks, envelopes, or anything that doesn't need frequent access. Sliding the top insert left or right exposes a ~16 × 31 cm column of the bottom layer.

### Top Insert (Sliding Tray with Compartments)

Full-depth, narrower than bottom insert. Slides left-to-right inside the bottom insert walls.

| Dimension | Value | Notes |
|:----------|:------|:------|
| Width     | 58 cm | Leaves ~16 cm travel inside the 74 cm bottom insert |
| Depth     | 31 cm | Full depth — matches bottom insert |
| Wall height | 6 cm | Fills remaining drawer height (12 − 5 − ~1 gap) |

> **Sliding travel:** The top insert can slide ~16 cm left or right. This exposes a column of the bottom insert for quick access without lifting the top tray. The insert always stays fully within the drawer — no overhang risk.

## Construction Guide

### Materials

- **Corrugated cardboard** (double-wall preferred for rigidity)
- Box cutter or craft knife
- Metal ruler (at least 40 cm)
- Hot glue gun or PVA glue
- Pencil for marking

### Cutting List — Bottom Insert

| Piece | Quantity | Dimensions (W × H) | Notes |
|:------|:--------:|:--------------------|:------|
| Base  | 1        | 74 × 31 cm          | Floor of the tray |
| Long wall  | 2  | 74 × 5 cm           | Front and back walls |
| Short wall | 2  | 29 × 5 cm           | Side walls (31 − 2×1 cm for cardboard thickness) |

### Cutting List — Top Insert

| Piece | Quantity | Dimensions (W × H) | Notes |
|:------|:--------:|:--------------------|:------|
| Base  | 1        | 58 × 31 cm          | Floor of the sliding tray |
| Long wall  | 2  | 58 × 6 cm           | Front and back walls |
| Short wall | 2  | 29 × 6 cm           | Side walls |
| Divider A  | 1  | 21 × 6 cm           | Between pens and cables (at 10 cm from left) |
| Divider B  | 1  | 21 × 6 cm           | Between cables and paper (at 28 cm from left) |
| Cross divider | 1 | 56 × 6 cm          | Separates front compartments from flex zone (at 21 cm from front) |

### Assembly Order

1. **Bottom insert**: Glue the four walls around the base. Let dry.
2. **Top insert base**: Lay out the base (58 × 31 cm).
3. **Outer walls**: Glue the four outer walls around the top insert base.
4. **Cross divider**: Glue the 56 cm cross divider at 21 cm from the front edge. This separates the three compartments from the flex zone.
5. **Vertical dividers**: Glue Divider A at 10 cm from the left wall, and Divider B at 28 cm from the left wall (i.e., 18 cm gap between A and B).
6. **Optional**: Add small felt pads or strips on the bottom of the top insert for smooth sliding.
7. **Let dry completely** before placing in drawer.

### Tips

- Score (don't cut through) cardboard to make clean fold lines for walls.
- Reinforce corners with extra cardboard strips or hot glue.
- If using single-wall cardboard, double up wall pieces for rigidity.
- Line compartment bottoms with felt or shelf liner for a polished finish.
- Add felt or wax paper strips on the bottom insert floor (where the top tray slides) to reduce friction.
- Adjust the cardboard thickness constant (~1 cm assumed here for double-wall) based on your actual material.
